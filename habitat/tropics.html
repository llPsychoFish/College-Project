Forest Habitat

Forest are vital ecosystems, home to over 80% of land animals.

What is a Forest?

A Forest is a large area dominated by trees and vegetation. Forests provide shelter, food, and breeding grounds for animals, birds, and insects. They also help maintain the earth's clmate and oxygen supply.

Types of Forests

Tropical Rainforest

Found near the equator, tropical rainforests are hot, humid, and home to animals like Jaguars, toucans, and frogs

Temperate Forest

These forests have four seasons and house animals like deer, wolves, and bears. They're found in North America and Europe.

Boreal Forest

Located in cold northern regions, boreal forests are filled with conifers and animals like moose, lynx, and owls

Animals in Forests

Forests supports a wide variety of animals depending on the type and location. Some common forest animals include:

  • Tigers, Leopards, and Elephants (Tropical Forest)
  • Foxes, Bears, and Racoons (Temperate Forests)
  • Wolves, Lynx, and Reindeer (Boreal Forest )
