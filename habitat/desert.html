Forest Habitat

Forests are rich ecosystems that cover 31% of the earth and , support more than 80% of all land animals.

What is a Forest?

A Forest is a vast area covered with tree,shrubs,and rich plant life. Forests regulate the climate, store carbon, purify water, provides food and offer habitat to countless species including birds, mammals, reptiles and insects. They also help maintain the earth's oxygen supply.

Types of Forests

Tropical Rainforest

Found near the equator, tropical rainforests are hot, humid, and home to animals like Jaguars, toucans, and frogs

Temperate Forest

These forests have four seasons and house animals like deer, wolves, and bears. They're found in North America,Asia and Europe.

Boreal Forest

Located in cold northern regions, boreal forests are filled with conifers and animals like moose, lynx, and owls

Montane Forest

Found in mountain regions with cooler temperatures. supports unique high-altitudes wildlife.

Animals in Forests

Lion

The king of the tropical forest,stealthy,and strong.Lions are endangered and need large territories.

Deer

Graceful and quiet, deer thrive in temperate and boreal forests, feeding on leaves and shrubs.

Monkey

Social and intelligent animals that live in the canopies of tropical rainforests.
